# Budget Approval Policy

Document owner: Finance Operations  
Audience: Department heads, cost center managers, procurement requesters  
Classification: Confidential  
Effective date: 2026-01-20

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines approval requirements for operating expenses, capital expenses, project spend, and budget transfers. It is intended to ensure that spending is authorized, recorded against the correct cost center, supported by business purpose, and reviewed before the company commits funds.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to purchase requisitions, purchase orders, supplier invoices without purchase orders, software subscriptions, professional services, travel programs, equipment purchases, project budgets, and budget transfers between cost centers.

Payroll, employee benefits, taxes, treasury transactions, intercompany settlements, and board-approved transactions follow separate policies unless Finance directs otherwise.

## 3. Approval Thresholds

Requests up to USD 2,500 may be approved by the cost center manager when budget is available and the purchase is not restricted.

Requests from USD 2,501 to USD 25,000 require cost center manager approval and department head approval.

Requests from USD 25,001 to USD 100,000 require department head approval, Finance Business Partner review, and Procurement review when a supplier is involved.

Requests above USD 100,000 require Finance Director approval and executive sponsor approval. Multi-year commitments are evaluated using total contract value, not only the first-year amount.

Any request involving legal, HR, security, regulated data, customer commitments, or a new vendor may require additional review regardless of amount.

## 4. Required Evidence

Each request must include business purpose, cost center, budget line, expected benefit, supplier name if known, quote or estimate, contract reference if applicable, delivery date, and the owner responsible for confirming receipt.

Project spend must reference an approved project code and current project budget. Capital spend must identify asset category, useful life, installation location, and whether ongoing operating costs are expected.

## 5. Segregation of Duties

The requester may not be the only approver. A person who approves a purchase must not approve receipt of their own goods or services unless an exception is documented and approved by Finance.

Vendor creation, bank-account changes, purchase approval, goods receipt, and payment release must be separated where practical. Finance must monitor compensating controls when small teams cannot fully separate duties.

## 6. Budget Transfers

Budget transfers between cost centers require approval from both cost center managers and Finance Business Partner review. Transfers cannot be used to bypass approval thresholds, split purchases, hide overspend, or fund restricted purchases.

## 7. Exceptions

Emergency purchases may proceed when delay would create a safety, legal, security, or critical operational risk. The requester must document the reason, obtain verbal approval from the accountable leader when possible, and complete retrospective approval within two business days.

## 8. Audit Evidence

Finance retains approval records, purchase orders, budget transfer forms, invoices, receipts, exception approvals, and payment confirmations according to the records schedule. Missing evidence may result in payment hold, budget correction, or management escalation.

## 9. Reference Basis

This internal sample policy is aligned with common procurement, budget control, segregation-of-duties, and public-sector invoice verification practices.
