# Employee Onboarding Policy

Document owner: Human Resources  
Audience: Hiring managers, HR operations, IT support, new employees  
Classification: Confidential  
Effective date: 2026-02-12

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the onboarding process for employees and long-term contractors. Effective onboarding ensures that new joiners are legally cleared to work, prepared with equipment and access, introduced to the organization, trained on required policies, and connected with their team.

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

Human Resources owns the onboarding case, employment paperwork, background-check coordination, orientation registration, benefits information, policy acknowledgments, and personnel records.

The hiring manager owns role expectations, team introduction, first-week plan, work assignments, buddy assignment, performance expectations, and ongoing check-ins.

IT owns account creation, device preparation, standard software, MFA enrollment, equipment shipping or desk setup, and first-day technical support.

Facilities owns workspace, badge access, building instructions, and health and safety information where applicable.

## 3. Pre-Boarding

Pre-boarding begins when the offer is accepted. HR must confirm start date, worker type, manager, location, employment eligibility steps, and required documentation. The manager must submit equipment and access requests at least five business days before the start date when possible.

IT must prepare a company-managed device, default productivity tools, email, collaboration access, and security controls. Access to restricted applications requires separate approval from the application owner and must follow least-privilege principles.

## 4. First Day

On the first day, the new joiner completes identity verification, payroll setup, badge or remote-access setup, MFA enrollment, device login, mandatory policy acknowledgments, and orientation registration.

The manager should welcome the employee, explain the team mission, review the first-week schedule, introduce the buddy, and confirm how the employee should ask for help.

## 5. First Week

The first week focuses on role clarity and safe access. The manager must review responsibilities, expected outcomes, key stakeholders, recurring meetings, communication norms, working hours, and immediate priorities.

The employee must complete mandatory training for security awareness, privacy, code of conduct, harassment prevention where applicable, records handling, and any role-specific compliance requirements.

## 6. First 30 Days

By day 30, the employee should understand core systems, team workflows, escalation paths, performance expectations, and near-term goals. The manager must hold a check-in to discuss progress, blockers, training needs, and whether access remains appropriate.

Temporary access granted for onboarding must be reviewed and removed if no longer needed.

## 7. First 90 Days

By day 90, the manager must confirm whether the employee is meeting expectations, whether additional training is needed, and whether the role scope has changed. HR may request completion status for required training and acknowledgments.

## 8. Records

HR retains onboarding documentation, policy acknowledgments, training completion, and eligibility records. IT retains device assignment, access approvals, and account provisioning logs.

## 9. Reference Basis

This internal sample policy is aligned with public onboarding guidance covering pre-boarding, first-day setup, orientation, manager responsibilities, buddy support, and first-90-day integration.
