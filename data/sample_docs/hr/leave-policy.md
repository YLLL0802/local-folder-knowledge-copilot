# Employee Leave Policy

Document owner: Human Resources  
Audience: Employees, managers, HR operations  
Classification: Confidential  
Effective date: 2026-01-25

## 1. Purpose

This policy describes how employees request vacation, sick leave, family care leave, bereavement leave, unpaid leave, and other approved absences. It is intended to support employee wellbeing, business continuity, fair scheduling, and accurate recordkeeping.

## 2. Vacation Leave

Employees may use vacation leave for rest, personal business, travel, emergencies, or other personal reasons. Vacation must be requested in the HR system before the absence unless an emergency prevents advance notice.

Managers are responsible for approving the timing of vacation based on business needs, team coverage, previously approved leave, critical deadlines, and fairness across the team. Managers should respond to requests promptly and should not leave requests pending without explanation.

Employees should plan vacation throughout the year rather than waiting until year end. Teams with seasonal blackout periods must publish them in advance and apply them consistently.

## 3. Sick Leave

Sick leave may be used for the employee's own medical needs, preventive care, recovery, mental health needs, family care, bereavement where allowed, and other legally protected purposes. Employees must notify their manager as soon as practical when they cannot work due to illness or medical need.

For absences longer than three consecutive workdays, HR may request medical certification or other administratively acceptable evidence, subject to applicable law. Managers must not ask for unnecessary medical details. Medical documentation must be handled by HR and stored separately from general personnel files.

## 4. Family and Caregiver Leave

Employees may request leave to care for an eligible family member, bond with a new child, support adoption or foster placement, or handle serious family medical needs. HR will determine eligibility, documentation requirements, pay status, and job protection based on law, company policy, and local practice.

Managers must coordinate with HR before denying or modifying leave that may be legally protected.

## 5. Bereavement Leave

Employees may request bereavement leave following the death of an eligible family member or other significant relationship recognized by local policy. Managers should handle bereavement requests with discretion and route questions to HR.

## 6. Unpaid Leave

Unpaid leave may be approved when paid leave is unavailable or when the situation does not qualify for paid leave. HR review is required before unpaid leave is approved so payroll, benefits, legal protections, and return-to-work expectations are clear.

## 7. Manager Responsibilities

Managers must review leave requests promptly, maintain team coverage, protect employee privacy, avoid retaliation, and consult HR when a request involves medical, family, disability, accommodation, or protected-leave issues.

Managers must not pressure employees to work while on approved medical or protected leave unless HR confirms that limited contact is appropriate.

## 8. Recordkeeping

Leave balances, requests, approvals, denials, supporting documentation, and changes must be recorded in approved HR systems. HR retains records according to the records schedule and applicable law.

## 9. Reference Basis

This internal sample policy is aligned with public HR leave guidance, including vacation scheduling, sick leave uses, evidence requirements, and agency recordkeeping concepts.
