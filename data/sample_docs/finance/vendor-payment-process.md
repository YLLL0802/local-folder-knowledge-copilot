# Vendor Payment Process

Document owner: Accounts Payable  
Audience: Finance, procurement requesters, cost center managers  
Classification: Confidential  
Effective date: 2026-02-05

## 1. Purpose

This process explains how supplier invoices are received, reviewed, approved, and paid. It is designed to reduce duplicate payments, prevent payment fraud, confirm receipt of goods or services, and maintain accurate financial records.

## 2. Vendor Master Data

Payments may only be issued to suppliers recorded in the approved vendor master. New vendors require Procurement review, tax documentation, sanctions screening if applicable, business justification, and approval by the vendor master data team.

Bank-account changes must be verified using a trusted contact method already stored in the vendor master, not the contact details included in the change request. The person verifying the change must record the date, method, contact, and outcome.

## 3. Invoice Intake

Invoices must be submitted to the Accounts Payable mailbox or supplier portal. Invoices sent directly to employees must be forwarded to Accounts Payable without editing. The invoice must show supplier name, invoice number, invoice date, amount, tax if applicable, purchase order or contract reference, remittance details, and description of goods or services.

Invoices missing required information are returned to the supplier or placed on hold until corrected.

## 4. Matching and Receiving

Purchase-order invoices require matching against the purchase order, receipt, and invoice. Goods must be received in the procurement system before payment is released. Services must be confirmed by the business owner according to the contract or statement of work.

Invoices outside tolerance for quantity, price, tax, freight, or service period are routed for exception review. Accounts Payable must not force-match an invoice to clear an exception without documented approval from the business owner and Finance.

## 5. Non-PO Invoices

Non-PO invoices are allowed only for approved categories such as utilities, statutory fees, memberships, refunds, or other Finance-approved exceptions. The requester must provide business purpose, cost center, approver, and evidence that the goods or services were received.

Repeated non-PO invoices for planned supplier work must be escalated to Procurement so future spend can be placed under purchase order control.

## 6. Payment Runs

Accounts Payable schedules payment runs based on invoice due date, payment terms, cash forecast, and payment method. Early payment requires Finance approval unless it is part of an approved discount program.

Payment files are reviewed before release. The reviewer checks total value, unusual vendors, duplicate invoice numbers, bank-account changes, manual payments, and invoices approved through exception workflows.

## 7. Fraud Controls

Employees must treat urgent bank changes, requests to bypass purchase orders, pressure to pay before receipt, mismatched supplier names, and instructions to avoid known contacts as fraud indicators. Suspected fraud must be reported to Security and Finance leadership before payment.

No employee may rely on an email thread alone to approve a vendor banking change or release a payment.

## 8. Records and Audit

Accounts Payable retains invoice image, purchase order, receipt, approval history, exception notes, vendor verification evidence, payment confirmation, and cancellation notes. Audit requests must be fulfilled using records from approved systems, not personal mailboxes.

## 9. Reference Basis

This internal sample process is aligned with public invoice receipt guidance, receiving controls, purchase-order matching practices, and vendor payment fraud controls.
