# Office IT Support Policy

Document owner: IT Service Management  
Audience: All employees and contractors  
Classification: Internal Basic  
Effective date: 2026-02-01

## 1. Purpose

This policy explains how employees request IT support, how incidents are prioritized, and which responsibilities belong to employees, managers, and IT support teams. It applies to company-managed laptops, phones, collaboration tools, office networks, identity access, printers, and approved business applications.

## 2. Support Channels

Employees must submit routine support requests through the service desk portal. Email may be used when the portal is unavailable. Urgent security concerns must be reported through the security incident hotline or the dedicated phishing-reporting button.

Walk-up support is available for hardware issues, badge-access problems, and first-day onboarding needs. Walk-up support does not replace ticket creation; the support engineer must create or update a ticket so the work is auditable.

## 3. Priority Levels

Priority 1 incidents affect a critical business service, multiple departments, executive communications, payroll, customer operations, or security response. IT must begin triage immediately and provide updates every 30 minutes until service is restored or a workaround is confirmed.

Priority 2 incidents affect a team, location, or important business application with no reasonable workaround. IT targets same-business-day triage and regular updates.

Priority 3 requests affect one user or a non-critical function with a workaround available. Examples include software installation, peripheral setup, mailbox configuration, and routine access questions.

Priority 4 requests are planned service items such as equipment refresh, reporting access, shared mailbox creation, or procurement consultation.

## 4. Identity Verification

IT may not reset passwords, change MFA devices, grant privileged access, or disclose account information without verifying the requester's identity. Acceptable verification methods include company SSO approval, manager confirmation, video verification, or an in-person badge check.

Managers may request access for direct reports, but application owners remain responsible for approving access to restricted systems.

## 5. Device Standards

Only company-managed devices may access internal systems that contain confidential or restricted information unless an exception has been approved by Security. Devices must run supported operating systems, endpoint protection, disk encryption, and automatic patching.

Employees must not disable endpoint controls, share local administrator credentials, connect unknown USB storage devices, or install unlicensed software. Lost or stolen devices must be reported immediately, even if the employee believes the device was locked.

## 6. Software Requests

Software requests must identify business purpose, cost center, data classification, and whether the tool will process personal, customer, financial, or confidential information. IT may require Security, Privacy, Procurement, or Legal review before approval.

Free online tools are not automatically approved. Browser extensions, file converters, AI tools, and automation utilities can create data leakage risk and must follow the same review process when used for company information.

## 7. Security Incident Escalation

Employees must report suspected phishing, malware, credential exposure, lost devices, suspicious sign-in prompts, and unexpected file encryption immediately. IT support must preserve relevant evidence, avoid unnecessary changes to affected systems, and escalate to Security when compromise is suspected.

Security incidents are handled separately from routine support tickets. The incident lead owns containment, investigation, communications, recovery, and post-incident review.

## 8. Records

All tickets must include requester, affected asset, service, priority, actions taken, approvals, and closure notes. Tickets involving access changes or security incidents must retain evidence according to the records schedule.

## 9. Reference Basis

This internal sample policy is aligned with NIST incident response guidance, CISA incident response planning concepts, and common IT service management practices.
